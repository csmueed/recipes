Pizza Recipes

Here’s a breakdown of the foundational elements for any great pizza, starting with the dough. The heart of a classic recipe is a simple, slow-fermented dough made from high-protein flour, water, salt, and a tiny amount of yeast. Allowing this mixture to rest in the refrigerator for at least 24 hours develops complex flavor and a beautifully airy, chewy crust. When you’re ready, stretch the dough gently by hand rather than using a rolling pin to preserve those precious gas bubbles, which will give you that signature puffy, charred edge when baked at the highest temperature your oven can manage.

For the toppings, the philosophy is often “less is more” to ensure a crisp, non-soggy base. A classic Margherita is the perfect benchmark: a thin layer of crushed San Marzano tomatoes, fresh mozzarella (preferably the low-moisture kind for melting), a few fresh basil leaves, and a drizzle of extra-virgin olive oil. However, the beauty of pizza is its versatility—feel free to experiment with caramelized onions, spicy pepperoni, or earthy mushrooms. Just remember to blot any wet ingredients like fresh mozzarella or vegetables with a paper towel before topping to keep your crust perfectly crisp.

  1. Flour (bread or all-purpose)
  2. Yeast (active dry or instant)
  3. Salt
  4. Olive Oil
  5. Canned tomatoes (crushed or whole peeled)
