Burger Recipes

Here’s a breakdown of the foundational elements for any great burger, starting with the patty. The heart of a classic burger recipe is high-quality ground beef with a balanced fat content, ideally around 80/20. Gently shape the meat into patties without overworking it, as this keeps the texture tender and juicy. Season generously with salt and pepper just before cooking, then grill or sear over high heat to develop a rich, flavorful crust while keeping the inside moist and delicious.

For the toppings, the philosophy is often “balance is key” to ensure every bite is satisfying. A classic cheeseburger is the perfect benchmark: a toasted bun, a juicy beef patty, melted cheddar cheese, crisp lettuce, fresh tomato slices, onions, pickles, and your favorite sauce. However, the beauty of burgers is their versatility—feel free to experiment with bacon, sautéed mushrooms, avocado, or spicy jalapeños. Just remember not to overload the burger with too many wet ingredients, as this can make the bun soggy and difficult to eat.

  1. Ground beef (80/20 lean-to-fat ratio)
  2. Burger buns
  3. Cheddar cheese slices
  4. Lettuce and tomato
  5. Pickles and burger sauce
